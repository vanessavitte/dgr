Steps to Becoming a Home Owner

Knowing what to expect during the home-buying process will allow you to enjoy the adventure and to better understand the steps that are needed in buying a home.

  1. Prequalify for a loan- Before you start considering anything else about home buying, it's important to understand approxi mately how much a lender will be willing to lend you in the form of a mortgage.
  2. Figure out what you want versus what you need - This will save you enormous amount of time. Make a list of what you most want in your dream home as well as the minimum monthly house payment you are willing to accept.
  3. Select a specific area- The more specific you can be about where you want to live, the faster everything will fall into place.
  4. Find a good Realtor/Agent - Most people choose to work with a real estate professional when buying their first home. A good agent will do everything from providing you with a list of houses that meet your criteria, to isolating those properties that fulfill your needs. A good agent will show you properties, assist you in making an offer on the home you want to buy, help you navigate through the paperwork, and finally, close the deal. Taking the time to find the right agent pays off in the long run.
  5. Look at properties - At this stage you will begin viewing selected properties based on your requirements and price range.
  6. Pick the right house - The next step is to narrow your choices by using the list of properties you've already selected, and asking your agent to provide a comparatice market analysis (CMA) on your list of properties. Eventually, you will be ready to make an offer on a home that is right for you.
  7. Negotiate the best deal and make an offer - Here is where your agent's skills and experience really comes in handy. Enlist their help when it's time to determine the right price to offer on your "dream home". Depending on the current housing market, your dream home may easily be yours.
  8. Arrange for a home inspection- Termites? Foundation problems? Problems with the air conditioning? These are potential problems you want to know about before purchasing your "Dream Home". This is why hiring a home inspector is very important.
  9. Get financing- You may already have been preapproved for a loan before you started the home-buying process, but now it is time to finalize your financing. How much should your down payment be? Do you want a 15 or 30 year mortgage? Do you want an FHA loan or a Conventional loan? These are some reasons a loan officer's assistance is invaluable.
  10. Open Escrow- Escrow is a legal arrangement in which an asset, usually a substantial sum of money is given to an independent third party to be held in trust until the conditions of the real estate contract have been fulfilled. This is part of the Earnest Money process.
  11. Close the deal- You've never seen paperwork like the paperwork involved in purchasing a home. Don't let the sheer volume of documents intimidate you. Your agent will be by your side to help. At the end of it all, the keys are in your hands and you have fulfilled the "American Dream"!
